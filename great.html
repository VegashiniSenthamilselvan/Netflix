The Great Wall

"The Great Wall" is a fantasy action film that blends Chinese mythology with Western storytelling. It was released in December 2016 and directed by Zhang Yimou, known for his visually stunning films like "Hero" and "House of Flying Daggers." The film features an international cast, including Matt Damon, Jing Tian, Pedro Pascal, Willem Dafoe, and Andy Lau. The story is set in medieval China and revolves around the iconic Great Wall and its defense against monstrous creatures.

Plot Summary

Introduction:

The film begins with European mercenaries William Garin (Matt Damon) and Pero Tovar (Pedro Pascal) traveling through China in search of black powder, a powerful explosive. They are captured by the Nameless Order, a secretive Chinese military organization tasked with defending the Great Wall against mythical creatures known as the Tao Tei.

Rising Action:

As the mercenaries are taken to the Great Wall, they witness a massive battle between the Nameless Order and the Tao Tei, which are monstrous, reptilian creatures that attack in waves every sixty years. The Order is led by Commander Lin Mae (Jing Tian) and Strategist Wang (Andy Lau). The Europeans join forces with the Chinese to combat the Tao Tei, recognizing the greater threat these creatures pose to humanity.

Climax:

The film's climax centers on a desperate battle to prevent the Tao Tei from breaching the Wall and reaching the capital. The Queen Tao Tei is the controlling force behind the horde, and the humans devise a plan to kill her, thus neutralizing the threat. William, Lin Mae, and Tovar lead the charge, using both Western and Eastern tactics and technology.

Resolution:

After an intense final battle, the humans succeed in their mission, killing the Queen and saving the capital from destruction. The film concludes with William deciding to stay in China, having found a new purpose beyond his initial mercenary pursuits.

Context and Analysis

The film explores the interaction and cooperation between Western and Eastern cultures, highlighting the potential for unity in the face of a common enemy. Central to the story is the theme of heroism, with characters making significant sacrifices for the greater good. The Nameless Order exemplifies selflessness and dedication.The evolving relationships between the mercenaries and the Chinese warriors underscore the importance of trust and camaraderie in overcoming challenges.

nitially portrayed as a skilled but self-serving mercenary, William's character arc involves him developing a sense of duty and honor. His transformation is driven by his interactions with the Nameless Order and their unwavering commitment to protecting humanity. Commander Lin Mae is a strong and capable leader who exemplifies the virtues of bravery and leadership. Her character serves as a counterbalance to William, providing a model of selflessness and strategic thinking. "The Great Wall" emphasizes cultural exchange, showcasing how different traditions and knowledge can complement each other. The presence of Western characters in an Eastern setting serves to highlight both the contrasts and synergies between cultures.he casting of Matt Damon in a leading role sparked discussions about whitewashing and the need for more authentic representation of Chinese characters in major roles. However, the film also aimed to be a bridge between Eastern and Western audiences.

"The Great Wall" is a visually stunning action film that brings together elements of Chinese mythology and Western storytelling. Through its epic battles and thematic exploration of heroism, sacrifice, and cultural unity, the film offers an entertaining, albeit sometimes superficial, cinematic experience. Despite mixed reviews and cultural criticisms, it remains a notable attempt at blending Hollywood action with Chinese epic filmmaking.
